Speech On Teachers Day Celebration for Students and Children in English

August 31, 2021 by Prasanna

Speech On Teachers Day Celebration: Education and wisdom form the basis of all accomplishments in life. Teachers have the power to impart education and shape a youth’s mind.

A child is turned into a responsible citizen by the aid of a teacher’s knowledge. They enhance our possibilities of having a bright and promising future. Teachers are the role models that we look up to and emulate.

Teachers’ Day is a day solely dedicated to teachers. It is a day when the teaching profession and a teacher’s efforts are honored and appreciated.

Students can also find more English Speech Writing about Welcome Speeches, Farewell Speeches, etc.

Long And Short Speeches On Teachers Day Celebration In English For Kids And Students

We are providing a long Speech On Teachers’ Day Celebration of 500 words and a short Speech On Teachers’ Day Celebration of 150 words along with ten lines on the same topic for the readers.

The given speeches will be useful for students who might be requested to deliver a speech on Teachers’ Day and its celebrations.

A Long Speech On Teachers Day Celebration is helpful to students of classes 7, 8, 9, 10, 11 and 12. A Short Speech On Teachers Day Celebration is helpful to students of classes 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6.

Long Speech on Teachers’ Day Celebration 500 words In English

Wishing a very good morning to my honorable Principal, my respected teachers, and my dear friends.

I am (your name), and I have been selected to deliver a speech on Teachers’ Day. I feel honored to get this opportunity to express my gratitude towards my teachers.

Teachers are our guides whom we look up to. On this day, I convey my best wishes to my teachers and welcome them with a round of applause. Teachers, on this day, take a break to enjoy and to celebrate themselves and their hard work.

Teachers’ Day is a day to appreciate and respect all the efforts put forward by a teacher. On this day, teachers from all over the world of different schools, colleges, and universities are honored. The date differs country wise.

Every year, the fifth of September is celebrated as Teachers’ Day in India to commemorate the birth anniversary of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, who was one of the most renowned, excellent and ideal teachers of India.

He was a scholar and received the Bharat Ratna in 1952. In 1954 he was awarded the German “Order pour le Merite for Arts and Science.” He was the first Vice President and second President of independent India.

The occasion of Teachers’ Day started when some of his students requested him to celebrate his birthday, instead he wanted his birthday to be remembered as Teachers’ Day.

World Teachers’ Day is celebrated on the fifth of October, and it started in 1994. UNESCO took the step to celebrate the teachers’ accomplishments and the hard work they incorporated in the education field.

Teachers form the building blocks of our lives. They stand as sturdy pillars supporting every student. Teachers serve selflessly in making us grow up into responsible human beings. They are that ray of hope in the dark that encourages us to survive. Teachers never consider us any less than their children. They are our other parent who takes our full responsibility in helping us grow.

Teachers are our guides, and they always stand by us, preparing us to tackle every tough situation that life challenges us with. An ideal and perfect teacher is not one who imparts knowledge, but an ideal teacher is the one who inspires each student to follow their dreams.

Students are tough to handle at times, but every teacher has helped us learn from our mistakes and grow. Teachers have scolded us for our wrongdoings and praised us whenever we managed to accomplish any milestone.

We all have imitated our teachers, one day or the other, because deep down somewhere, every student wants to become like their role model, and some wish to gain certain qualities of their favorite teachers as well.

On this Day before commencing the Teachers’ Day celebrations, I would like to thank my teachers from the bottom of my heart for standing beside us and helping us grow. The students hold no value without their teachers.

Thank You.

Short Speech On Teachers’ Day Celebration 150 Words In English

Wishing everyone, my honorable Principal, my respected teachers, and my dear friends very good morning.

I am (your name), and I have been selected to deliver a speech on Teachers’ Day celebrations, and I am grateful for receiving this opportunity to thank my teachers for their tireless efforts.

We celebrate Teachers’ Day in the memory of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, a teacher par excellence, a role model for many, and one of India’s successful Presidents.

Teachers’ Day celebrates and honors every teacher, not only in the education field but in all premises of life. Teachers mold us and help us to reach our aims in life. The country’s future lies in the hand of teachers since they are the ones that impart knowledge and guide students to become honest and responsible citizens.

Today I want to express my sincere gratitude towards all my teachers for making us what we are today.

Thank You.

  2. In India, Teachers’ Day is celebrated to honour the memory of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan on fifth of September and this occasion of Teachers’ Day began in 1962.
  3. Teachers Day is celebrated in all educational institutions where the teachers are showered with flowers and gifts by their students who express their thankfulness, and it is one of the most celebrated occasions.
  4. Bhutan celebrates Teachers’ Day on the second of May to commemorate the birth anniversary of the third king and China celebrates Teachers’ Day on the eleventh of October.
  5. A worldwide celebration of Teachers’ Day takes place on the fifth of October put forward by UNESCO.

  10. A teacher is always called the captain of the ship because it is because of them, we can reach our destinations and fulfill our dreams.

FAQ’s On Speech On Teachers’ Day Celebration

Question 1.
On which date is Teachers’ Day celebrated worldwide?

Answer:
Worldwide Teachers’ Day is celebrated on the fifth of October.

Question 2.
Who was Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan?

Answer:
Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan was an Indian philosopher, an academic and statesman, and an excellent teacher who served India as the first Vice President and the second President.

Question 3.
An ideal teacher should consist of what personalities?

Answer:
An ideal teacher should tackle students calmly. They should not only impart knowledge, but a perfect teacher should always be ready to gain experience through all possible means because the country’s future depends upon how a teacher nurtures his or her students.

Question 4.
Since which year, India celebrates Teachers’ Day?

Answer:
India celebrates Teachers Day since 1962 in the memory of the birth anniversary of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan.
